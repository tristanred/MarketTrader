Top-10 default + expand widget. Showing 31 rows inline would dominate the page; showing only the leader would hide context. The compromise: top 10 visible by default, with a chip-styled ▾ Show all 31 players button at the bottom of the panel. The hint text inside the button ("21 hidden · including you @ #25") tells you both what you're missing and where you are — the second part is what makes the collapsed state usable for players outside the top 10. The cyan tint preview below shows the expanded state: rows 11–31 rendered in the same row format, with your row highlighted in its natural position.

Why the centre column. The current left rail is ~280px and usernames already truncate (+3.8… in the screenshot). Moving the leaderboard into the centre column under Holdings unlocks ~860px of width, which is enough for: full username (24+ chars), a 240×24 sparkline that reads as a trend, value, %P&L, and a Δ24h column.

Two-tier find-me. Tier 1: the pinned "▸ YOU" row directly under the header is always visible regardless of collapse state — so even when only top-10 is shown, your row is still on screen. Tier 2: when expanded, your row also renders in its natural rank position, giving you the "who's right above/below me" context. Both use --accent and a heavier sparkline stroke.

Header chip group. Range chips (1D · 5D · 10D · ALL) — fewer than before; dropped 20D since the typical game length is ≤30 days and 10D/ALL covers it. Full view ↗ deep-links to Option 02. ● LIVE pulses as today.

Designed for 25+ players. A naïve 31-line spaghetti chart is unreadable. Three rules tame it: (1) Default view = "Top 10 + you" — only 11 colored lines, plus your --accent series always pinned. (2) The other 21 players collapse into a faded grey "field band" (5th-95th percentile envelope, see the soft grey shape) so you can still see how the pack moved without naming every line. (3) The series-toggle row lets you mute/unmute individual players; "+ show 21 more" un-collapses the field band. "View" modes add %P&L (normalises every player to 0% at start) and Rank (Y axis = rank position, so overtakes become explicit X-crossings).

Your row stays findable. The "▸ you · #25" label at the right edge of the chart, a dedicated "Your row, expanded" card with Δ-to-#1 and Δ-to-#24 metrics, and the standings-table highlight all keep you anchored even in a 31-player field.

Plan summary

01 reclaims real estate by moving the Leaderboard from the cramped 280px left rail into the centre column under Holdings — 860px of width unlocks per-row sparklines and full usernames. A pinned "▸ YOU" row keeps the user anchored even when their natural rank is #25 of 31. 02 takes everything that doesn't fit in the centre-column panel and gives it a home: full-size race chart with smart series-management for 25+ players, podium, full standings table, tournament stats, movers, highlights, exports.

  • Layout change in arena: drop Leaderboard from left rail; add it to centre column under HoldingsPanel. Watchlist/Activity migrate to free left-rail space.
  • Default: top 10 + you (pinned). Collapse widget at panel footer expands to the full field. Hint inside the button names your rank so the collapsed state is still useful for non-top-10 players.
  • Shared API addition: GET /games/:id/leaderboard/history?range=1d|5d|10d|all → { playerId, points: [{ t, value }] }[]. One endpoint feeds sparklines and the full chart.
  • Snapshot cadence: 5-min portfolio-value snapshots server-side, aligned with the WS price-batch interval already in CLAUDE.md.
  • Reusable components: <PortfolioSparkline> for in-row 240×24, <PortfolioRaceChart> for the dedicated page. Both consume the same history payload.
  • Colour slots: deterministic hash(playerId) → --p2..--p8; current user is always --accent so "your line" is constant.
  • Find-me affordances: pinned top row in centre-column panel; right-edge label + standings highlight + you-card on dedicated page.
  • Crowd handling on the chart: default Top-10+you view; muted "field band" envelope for the rest; togglable per series.
